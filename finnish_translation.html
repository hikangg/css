Finnish Translation

Finnish translation is offered to natives of Finland or to companies with operation in The Republic of Finland, also known as Finland, a country located in Northern Europe. The Finnish refer to themselves as "Suomalaiset," "Tavastians," "Hämäläiset," and "Karelians" or "Karjalaiset." The Finnish-speaking segment of the Finnish population, which is the majority of the population in Finland are referred to as the Finns.

There are two distinct groups of Finnish dialects, namely Western and Eastern. It is worth noting that the dialects are mutually intelligible. What distinguish them from each other are only minor differences in diphthongs, rhythm and vowels. Most of Finland and Satakunta speak the south-west dialects, while the Tavastian dialects are spoken in Tavastia. Abbreviation of word-final vowels, similar to Estonian, is their classic trait.
