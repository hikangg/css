Swahili Translation

'Swahili' is derived from the Arabic word 'Sawahil,' which signifies "coastal dwellers." In 1012 A.D, Islam was known in the East African which explains why Swahili people are Muslim in the very strict and traditional form of Islam. Swahili (Kiswahili) is a Bantu language and the official language of 4 nations.

The Arabic language contributed to approximately 35% of the Swahili vocabulary; however, Swahili alphabet is identical to that of English, despite the absence of X and Q in the Swahili alphabet.
