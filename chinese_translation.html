Chinese Translation

Chinese translation is primarily offered to people from China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Macau, Singapore and Malaysia. There are two types of Chinese translations: Simplified Chinese translation and Traditional Chinese. Translation to Traditional system is required for people or businesses operating in Hong Kong, Taiwan, Macau and Chinese-speaking communities outside of Mainland China. Translation with Simplified Chinese characters, on the other hand, is used in Mainland China, Singapore and Malaysia. Simplified Chinese was developed in mainland China in order to increase literacy by making the characters faster to write and easier to memorize.

Chinese Translation

There are between seven and thirteen main regional groups of spoken Chinese, with Mandarin being the most widely used and "standardized." Mandarin is commonly the spoken standard for Chinese. The most common spoken dialects are Mandarin (about 850 million speakers), Wu (90 million), Cantonese and Min (each with about 70 million speakers). Standard Mandarin is the official language of the People's Republic of China (PRC) and the Republic of China (ROC). It is also an official language of Singapore, but not the primary language.
